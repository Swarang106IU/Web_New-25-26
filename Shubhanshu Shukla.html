Childhood and Early Education

Shubhanshu Shukla, a native of Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, completed his schooling at City Montessori School, Aliganj. He completed his military training and earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science from the National Defence Academy in 2005.He was then selected for the flying branch and underwent training at the Indian Air Force Academy. In June 2006, he was commissioned into the Fighter stream of the Indian Air Force as a Flying officer.

Indian Air Force and Astronout Career

He is a combat leader and seasoned test pilot with about 2,000 hours of flying experience in various aircraft, including the Su-30 MKI, MiG-21, MiG-29, Jaguar, Hawk, Dornier 228, and An-32.Shukla was shortlisted as one of four astronauts (along with Prasanth Balakrishnan Nair, Ajit Krishnan, and Angad Pratap) for India’s Gaganyaan (Hindi: “celestial vehicle”) mission, which plans to send as many as three astronauts into low Earth orbit in India’s first crewed flight, H1, likely in 2025. India would be the fourth country to launch its own crewed spacecraft, after Russia, the United States, and China.

Axiom 4 Space Mission

Axiom 4 is the fourth commercial human spaceflight mission undertaken by Axiom Space. The company collaborated with India, Poland, and Hungary for the mission. Comdr. Peggy Whitson (U.S.), mission specialist Sławosz Uznański-Wiśniewski (a European Space Agency astronaut from Poland), and mission specialist Tibor Kapu (Hungary) are mission pilot Shukla’s crew members for the Ax-4 mission.

Role of Shubhanshu In Axiom 4 Misson

In August 2024 the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) announced that Shukla would be the mission pilot for the Ax-4 mission to the ISS. The announcement came after an agreement was signed between ISRO’s Human Space Flight Centre and Axiom Space, a private aerospace company. ISRO mentioned in its announcement that Gaganyaan astronaut Nair would be the backup pilot for the mission.Shukla is set to be the first Indian national to travel to the ISS and is the second Indian national to travel to space, more than 40 years after wing commander Rakesh Sharma traveled to the space station Salyut 7 along with two Soviet cosmonauts.
